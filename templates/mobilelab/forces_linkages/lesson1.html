Lesson 1 — Forces & Motion in Linkages

Background Reading

Many everyday tools — scissors, door hinges, even robot arms — use connected bars and pins called linkages. These parts transfer motion and force from one place to another.

Engineers use Newton’s Laws of Motion to explain how linkages move and react:

  • 1st Law (Inertia) – Things stay still or keep moving the same way until a force changes them.
  • 2nd Law (Force = mass × acceleration) – The harder you push, the faster it speeds up.
  • 3rd Law (Action–Reaction) – Every push creates an equal push back in the opposite direction.

Vocabulary: force = a push or pull · motion = change in position · linkage = connected moving bars · effort = how hard you push · resistance = how much it pushes back.
